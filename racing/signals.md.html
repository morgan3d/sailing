**Sailboat Race Committee Signals** Published 2023-09-10; Updated 2023-09-10 This article describes the sound signals and flags used by a sailboat race committee under the 2021-2024 [racing rules of sailing (RRS)](https://www.sailing.org/inside-world-sailing/rules-regulations/racing-rules-of-sailing/). Postponement ==================================================================== Abandonment ==================================================================== Rules in Effect ==================================================================== Start ==================================================================== Recalls -------------------------------------------------------------------- Course Changes ==================================================================== Finish ==================================================================== All Flags ==================================================================== The flags for signaling during racing are the standard international maritime signal flags under the [International Code of Signals (ICS)/INTERCO](https://msi.nga.mil/api/publications/download?key=16694273/SFH00000/Pub102bk.pdf&type=view), plus an orange and a blue flag. However, the flag _interpretation_ differs from the ICS, and the sound signals do not match the Morse code for the flags from ICS. In racing, the Code/Answer flag is referred to as the "Answering Pennant" and abbreviated "AP". The first repeater/Repeat 1 flag is referred to as the "General Recall" flag.
